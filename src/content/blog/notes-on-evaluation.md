Evaluation is where a lot of NLP work quietly succeeds or fails. A model that
looks great on one benchmark can fall apart the moment the distribution shifts.

A few things I try to keep in mind:

1. **Know what the metric rewards.** Aggregate scores hide failure modes.
2. **Look at the data.** Read examples — both wins and losses.
3. **Report variance.** A single run is an anecdote, not a result.

> The goal isn't a higher number; it's understanding *why* the number moved.
